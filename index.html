Day 09
What is a Pullback?
▼
Story

In an uptrend, price goes up... then takes a small rest backwards... then continues up. That rest = pullback. It's the boss taking a breath before the next push.

How to See It

Trend is UP → Price drops (pullback) → Price resumes UP. Same for downtrends: DOWN → bounces up → continues DOWN. Pullbacks are temporary moves AGAINST the main trend.

Pullback Pattern — The Visual Story
CRITICAL: Don't Confuse These

Pullback: Small dip WITHIN trend, then resume. Reversal: Trend completely changes direction. Beginners see pullback = think reversal = sell the uptrend. This is why they lose.

Day 10
Pullback Entry Rules
▼
The Setup

UPTREND → PULLBACK DOWN (2-4 candles) → STOP (pullback ends) → STRONG CANDLE UP (confirmation) → ENTRY.

What To Look For
Trend was clear and strong
Price pulls back 20-40% of the previous move
Pullback lasts 2-4 candles maximum
STRONG candle closes the pullback (big, near high)
Day 11
Where to Exit?

Story

STOP LOSS = right below the pullback low. TARGET = at least 1.5x the distance you risk. If you risk 10, you must make 15+.

Risk:Reward Rules
Risk = Entry to Stop Loss
Reward = Entry to Target
ONLY trade if Reward ≥ 1.5 × Risk
Never take 1:1 — bad risk management

Story

Day 15
What is a Trap?
▼
CRITICAL CONCEPT

A TRAP = price breakout that looks strong but IMMEDIATELY REVERSES. Traders who bought the breakout = TRAPPED = forced to exit = creates violent movement = YOUR PROFIT.

How Traps Work
Price breaks out ABOVE resistance (looks bullish)
Retail traders buy the breakout
Smart money was waiting here (with stops)
Sudden reversal STOPS trapped traders
They exit = violent sell-off = DOWN move
The Phrase

👉 "If breakout = TOO FAST + IMMEDIATE REVERSAL = TRAP"

🔥 THE TRAP FLOW (Most Important Visual in Course)
HOW THIS MAKES MONEY

Step 1: You see breakout starting. Step 2: You DON'T follow retail — you WAIT. Step 3: Reversal happens (trap confirmed). Step 4: You enter OPPOSITE direction. Step 5: Violent move creates 3:1 or 5:1 profit. Retail lost, you won.

Day 16
Liquidity Sweep & Stop Hunt
▼
Story

Smart traders know that most people put stops JUST BELOW the lows. They hunt these stops = create a spike through the low = catch stops = then reverse. This is called a LIQUIDITY SWEEP or STOP HUNT CANDLE.

The Pattern
Price is in uptrend, bouncing off support
Suddenly ONE candle spikes DOWN (long lower wick)
This spike STOPS trapped shorts (they exit)
Next candle REVERSES UP hard
Price continues the uptrend
TRAP CANDLE RULE

A stop hunt candle = long wick against the trend + immediate reversal. This is a STRONG signal that the trap caught shorts = uptrend continues.

Practice
Find 2 false breakouts (trap candles)
Find 2 stop hunt candles (spikes that reverse)
Mark what happened AFTER each trap
